# Contributing

This is a living resource. Contributions from clinicians, researchers,
patient advocates, and anyone with relevant expertise are welcome.

## What we accept

### Established diagnostic entities

For a new entry under `DIFFERENTIAL/`, please include:

- A brief overview of the entity (1–2 paragraphs)
- Key clinical features that should prompt consideration
- The specific diagnostic test(s), with sample type (blood, CSF, tissue)
- Notes on availability or cost when known, particularly for
  resource-limited settings
- At least one peer-reviewed primary source citation

### Hypotheses

For an entry under `HYPOTHESES/`, please include:

- Explicit framing as a hypothesis (not as established fact)
- The chain of reasoning
- Supporting literature with full citations
- **Acknowledged limitations and contrary evidence**
- Proposed empirical tests that could falsify or support the hypothesis
- Disclosure of any direct conflict of interest

A hypothesis entry that overstates evidence will be revised in review.
The standard for inclusion is honest framing, not novelty.

### Case patterns

Anonymized clinical patterns are welcome but **must not contain any
patient-identifying information**: no names, no initials, no exact
dates of birth, no rare combinations of features that could identify
the patient, no images that could allow re-identification. Approximate
ages (decade), region (country or larger), and time courses
(approximate months/years) are acceptable.

If a pattern is based on a real patient, the contributor must confirm
that either (a) appropriate consent or institutional permission was
obtained, or (b) the level of de-identification is such that re-
identification is implausible.

### Regional information

Cost and availability data should be cited (lab websites, published
cost analyses, formulary documents) and dated. Healthcare landscapes
change; we will mark outdated information for revision.

## What we do not accept

- Treatment protocols or therapeutic regimens. This resource is
  diagnostic, not therapeutic. Treatment depends on the established
  diagnosis and is the responsibility of treating physicians.
- Patient-identifying information of any kind.
- Speculation framed as evidence.
- Promotional material for specific commercial labs or products.

## Process

1. Open an issue describing the proposed contribution before writing,
   so duplication is avoided and feedback is offered early.
2. Fork the repository, create a branch, and submit a pull request.
3. At least one maintainer will review. Specialty-specific
   contributions may be reviewed by an external clinician or
   researcher in that subspecialty.
4. Approved contributions are merged with appropriate attribution.

## Conduct

Contributions, issues, and discussion are expected to be respectful
and focused on the content. Disagreements about evidence are welcome
and productive when framed around the literature and reasoning rather
than the contributor.

## Authorship and credit

Contributors are acknowledged in the repository's contributors list
(auto-maintained by GitHub) and, for substantive contributions, in
periodic Zenodo releases. Authorship credit on derived publications
follows the conventions of the discipline and the journal.

## Licensing
